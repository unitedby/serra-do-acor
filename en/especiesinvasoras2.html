Invasive Species

PT

EN

The spread of silver wattle (Acacia dealbata), which has been controlled in the Mata da Margaraça area since 2004, as well as others such as blackwood (Acacia melanoxylon), black locust (Robinia pseudoacacia) and small-leaf spiderwort (Tradescantia fluminensis) may threaten riparian woodland habitats (91E0 and 92A0), as well as the mosaic of oak woods (9230), chestnut woods (9260) and mixed forests (9160) which are found in Mata da Margaraça.

According to the POAPPSA studies (ICN, 2007), the most notable invasive plants in the PPSA area are: Jimson weed (Datura stramonium L.), small-leaf spiderwort (Tradescantia fluminensis Velloso), prickly pear (Opuntia ficus-indica L.), tree-of-heaven (Ailanthus altissima Miller), black locust (Robinia pseudoacacia L.), Australian blackwood (Acacia melanoxylon R. Br.) and silver wattle (Acacia dealbata). These last two acacia species pose the greatest threat to the protected area. The presence of Ailanthus altissima in the area bordering Mata da Margaraça is a threat to the neighbouring forest habitat types, given how difficult this species is to eradicate.
