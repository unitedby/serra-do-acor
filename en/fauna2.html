Of these, 423 are invertebrate species (including 241 butterflies) and 117 are vertebrates. The group of vertebrates contains 7 amphibian species, 11 reptile species, 64 bird species, and 35 mammal species. Many of these species are covered by some form of protection status.

There is, for example, the butterfly Euplagia quadripunctaria and the Euphydryas aurinia (which is included in the Habitats Directive as a priority species for conservation). All amphibian species also have some protection status. Particularly noteworthy are the Lusitanian salamander (Chioglossa lusitanica), the Iberian frog (Rana iberica), and Bosca’s newt (Lissotriton boscai) because they are species endemic to the Iberian Peninsula and are considered vulnerable or otherwise threatened species.
Two reptiles are particularly significant in terms their protection status: Schreiber’s green lizard (Lacerta schreiberi) and Lataste’s viper (Vipera latastei).
