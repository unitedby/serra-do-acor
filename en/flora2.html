Serra do Açor

Vegetation and Flora

EN

There is a significant presence of rare species, with a restricted geographical distribution, or which reach the limit of their range here. Some of these species are protected under national and international legislation.

The Central Mountain Range marks the boundary between several biogeographical regions, where Mediterranean species coexist with more markedly Atlantic species. These influences can be seen, for example, in the difference between N-NW-facing slopes, where species with Atlantic and Eurosiberian distributions predominate (pedunculate oak, Pyrenean oak, sweet chestnut, broom, etc.), and S-SE-facing slopes, where Mediterranean plants predominate (strawberry tree, cork oak, holm oak, Cistus spp., etc.). Of the total recorded species, 30% are Mediterranean, 18% have Atlantic affinities, and 19% are endemic species (Iberian, Lusitanian, etc.).
