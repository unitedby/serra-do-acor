Fraga da Pena

EN

The Barroca de Degraínhos valley is of particular importance not only near Fraga da Pena and Pardieiros, but also upstream. Here it is possible to enjoy the natural and cultural features of this valley through the clearly marked interpretive walking trails on the ground, which follow the course of the stream and then continue towards Enxudro.

Despite the fires, the banks of the stream still preserve some old specimens of the pedunculate oak (Quercus robur), the Portugal laurel (Prunus lusitanica subsp. lusitanica), the sweet chestnut (Castanea sativa), the strawberry tree (Arbutus unedo), the laurustinus (Viburnum tinus), as well as the flax-leaved daphne (Daphne gnidium) and the green olive tree (Phillyrea latifolia). The special conditions offering shelter and the high air humidity at Fraga da Pena make this an excellent place for the development of several bryophyte species, particularly liverworts, and pteridophytes such as the Mediterranean clubmoss (Selaginella denticulata), the maidenhair spleenwort (Asplenium trichomanes), and the Jersey fern (Anogramma leptophylla). At Fraga da Pena there is a picnic area available to visitors. It is also possible to access two walking trails, one passing through the village of Pardieiros and another following the Barroca de Degraínhos and the Ribeira do Enxudro, both marked on the ground.

The most important waterfall, with a 19 metre drop, is part of a set of 10 waterfalls arranged in a cascade system, together forming an approximate drop of 65 metres. Its formation is due to the dual factors of the greater hardness of the local rock and the probable existence of small transverse faults.
