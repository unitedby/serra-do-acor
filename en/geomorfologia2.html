Serra do Açor lies in the Portuguese Central Mountain Range. In the 12th century this mountain range was considered a single unit known as the Serra do Ermio. Nowadays, three different ranges are recognized: Serra da Estrela, Serra do Açor, and Serra da Lousã.

The dominant rock in Serra do Açor is schist. The tectonic forces that formed the mountain range, together with the nature of this rock and the action of erosive agents, produced a landscape characterized by rounded forms and cut by steep-sided valleys. Folding and faulting created a vigorous but rounded characteristic relief, dissected by valleys with significant changes in elevation, incised watercourses, and, at times, unusual geological features such as the Fraga da Pena waterfalls.
